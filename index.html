Designing for All: Empowering Diverse Learners

This project empowers rural Chinese students with physical or learning variations to craft compelling U.S. college essays. Guided by Universal Design for Learning (UDL), it offers personalized, accessible modules and AI-assisted tools to support inclusive, equitable access to international education.

  • Application requirements: tests, GPA, rec letters
  • Unique features of top U.S. colleges
  • Overview of popular majors

U.S. colleges care about more than just your grades. They want to know: Who are you? What do you care about? How do you grow? You can show this in your essays, projects, and how you tell your story.

美国大学不仅仅看成绩。他们想了解：你是谁？你关心什么？你是如何成长的？你可以通过文书、项目以及讲述你自己的方式来展示这些内容。

1:10–2:00

Most applications include: School grades, English test (like TOEFL), Letters from teachers, Personal essays, Activities (even helping family counts!). Not all schools ask for all things. Don’t worry — we can help.

大多数申请包括：学校成绩、英语考试（如托福）、老师推荐信、个人文书、活动（即使是帮助家人也算！）。不是所有学校都要求全部内容。别担心——我们可以帮助你。

You don’t have to choose your major right away. You can try different classes. They support all kinds of students, including those with anxiety, ADHD, or language needs. You can learn by reading, building, or creating — your way.

你不需要一开始就决定专业。你可以尝试不同的课程。学校支持各种学生，包括有焦虑、多动症或语言困难的学生。你可以用自己的方式学习——无论是阅读、动手还是创作。

热门专业包括：工程、科学、心理学、文学与设计。你甚至可以自己设计专业。你关心什么，比专业名称更重要。

从小处开始。选择一个改变了你的时刻。用自己的语言讲述你的故事。你不需要完美，只要真实。需要帮助时请开口。你并不孤单。

0:00–0:40

Hello again! In this video, we’ll talk about different kinds of essay prompts you may see on U.S. college applications.
We’ll help you understand each type — and how to write in a way that’s true to you.

大家好！在这个视频中，我们将讲解美国大学申请中常见的文书题型。
我们会帮你理解每种题目类型——以及如何用真实的方式来写出你的故事。

0:40–2:00

Leadership Prompts: These questions ask about a time when you led or made a difference...
Focus on what you did, why it mattered, and how it changed you.

领导力题目： 这些题目会问你是否在某个时刻起到了带头作用或产生了影响。
重点在于你做了什么，为什么重要，以及你从中得到了什么成长。

2:00–3:20

Challenge or Obstacle Prompts: These ask about a difficulty you’ve faced...
Focus on how you responded — what did you learn, how did you grow?

挑战与障碍题目： 这些题目想知道你是否经历过困难。
重点是你如何应对——你学到了什么，怎样成长了？

3:20–4:30

Community or Volunteer Essays: These ask about your role in helping others or being part of something bigger...
What did you give? What did you receive? How did it shape you?

社区与志愿服务题目： 这些题目关注你是否参与了对他人或集体有意义的活动。
你付出了什么？得到了什么？这对你产生了怎样的影响？

4:30–5:30

Talent or Passion Essays: These let you show something you’re great at...
Why does it matter to you? How has it helped you grow or connect?

才艺与热情题目： 这类题目让你展示自己擅长或热爱的领域。
请讲故事：这项才艺对你意味着什么？它如何帮助你成长或与他人建立联系？

5:30–6:40

Academic Interest Prompts: These ask why you want to study a specific subject...
Then show how you’ve followed that interest.

学术兴趣题目： 这类题目会问你为什么对某个专业感兴趣。
然后讲讲你是如何延续这个兴趣的。

6:40–7:30

Personal Story Essays: These are open-ended. You get to choose the story...
What changed you? What value do you carry now?

个人故事题目： 这类题目较为开放，你可以自己选一个重要的经历来讲述。
什么事情改变了你？你现在最珍视的价值是什么？

7:30–8:00

Thanks for watching! Remember: you don’t need to impress — just be honest, thoughtful, and specific. Every student’s voice matters. Let your story lead the way.

感谢观看！记住：你不需要炫耀，只需要真诚、有思考、具体表达。
每位学生的声音都值得被听见。让你的故事带你走得更远。

  • Grammar: clear, simple, correct tense
  • Style: voice, variation, metaphor

0:00–0:40

Welcome! In this short video, we’ll cover how to write a strong, clear essay for your U.S. college application.
We’ll talk about structure, grammar, and style — all in simple steps.

欢迎你！在本视频中，我们将讲解如何写出清晰、有力的美国大学申请文书。
我们会一步步讲解结构、语法和表达技巧。

0:40–1:30

1. Structure: A strong essay usually has three parts:
- Introduction: Start with a hook...
- Body: Explain what happened, how you felt, what you learned.
- Conclusion: Tie it all together.

1. 结构： 一篇好的文书通常包括三个部分：
- 开头：用一个引人入胜的句子或瞬间作为“钩子”。
- 正文：讲清楚发生了什么、你有什么感受、你学到了什么。
- 结尾：总结，展示你的成长。

1:30–2:30

2. Grammar and Common Mistakes:
- Keep sentences short and clear.
- Use past tense for stories, present tense for reflections.
- Avoid big words — clear is better than fancy.
- Watch for errors: subject-verb agreement, run-ons, missing articles.

2. 语法和常见错误：
- 句子简短明了。
- 讲故事用过去式，表达感悟用现在式。
- 清晰比华丽更重要。
- 注意主谓一致、长句断句、冠词遗漏。

2:30–3:30

3. Style and Voice Tips:
- Be yourself — voice matters more than grammar.
- Use metaphors, details.
- Vary sentence length.
- Read aloud to check rhythm.

3. 风格与表达建议：
- 做自己。你的声音比语法更重要。
- 用比喻和细节让故事生动。
- 混合长短句，读出节奏。
- 大声朗读检查语感。

3:30–4:00

You don’t need to sound like a textbook. You just need to sound like you — thinking, growing, and ready to apply.
You’ve got this. Let’s keep writing!

你不需要像教科书一样写作。
你只要像你自己——在思考、成长、准备申请的你。
你可以做到的！继续写下去吧！
